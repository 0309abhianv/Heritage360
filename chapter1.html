Taj Mahal

An Eternal Symbol of love and Architectural Perfection

The Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World, stands majestically on the banks of the River Yamuna in Agra. It was commissioned in 1631 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth. The monument was completed in 1653, employing over 20,000 artisans from across India and Central Asia.

The Taj Mahal, made of white Makrana marble, is a masterpiece of symmetry and elegance spread across 42 acres. Its main mausoleum features a 73-meter dome with the tombs of Shah Jahan and Mumtaz Mahal adorned with precious stone inlays and Qur’anic inscriptions. Four minarets, each 40 meters tall, tilt slightly outward for protection during earthquakes. The Charbagh gardens with flowing water channels represent peace and paradise, while the reflecting pool mirrors the monument’s beauty. The red sandstone gateway, decorated with calligraphy, marks the grand entrance to this serene marvel.
